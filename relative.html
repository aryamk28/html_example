HAMPI

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, India, stands as a testament to the Vijayanagara Empire's grandeur. Once the empire's capital, Hampi is known for its magnificent ruins, elaborate temples, and intricate monuments, including the Virupaksha Temple. The site's diverse landscape, encompassing royal complexes, sacred shrines, and market streets, offers a glimpse into the rich cultural and architectural heritage of the Vijayanagara period.

TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal is a majestic white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It stands on the south bank of the Yamuna River and is a UNESCO World Heritage site, recognized for its exquisite Mughal architecture and as a symbol of love. The Taj Mahal was commissioned in 1632 and its construction took over 20 years to complete.

RED FORT

The Red Fort, located in Delhi, is a monumental structure built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century. Constructed primarily from red sandstone and marble, it's a striking example of Mughal architecture and served as the main residence for the Mughal emperors for nearly two centuries. The fort's massive walls, which stretch for 2.5 kilometers, and intricate design, featuring palaces, gardens, and ornate halls, have earned it recognition as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It's a significant historical landmark and a popular tourist destination.

CHARMINAR

Charminar is an iconic monument located in the heart of Hyderabad, India, known for its four grand minarets and intricate architectural design. Built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, it is a prime example of Indo-Islamic architecture and a popular tourist attraction. The structure features a mosque on the top floor, and the surrounding area is known for its bustling markets and vibrant culture.
